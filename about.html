INDIA / OUR_STORY

We got a $16,000 quote. We built $49.

VULNRA started from a single moment of frustration: an Indian startup founder who needed an LLM security audit, received a quote that was five months of runway, and decided to build the tool that should have already existed.

Origin

The problem
was the price.

In 2025, a fintech startup's enterprise client asked for an LLM security audit before signing a contract. The quote from a specialist firm: $16,000 USD. Two-week turnaround. Annual cadence. For a seed-stage startup, that's not a line item — it's a decision point.

The open-source tools that existed required deep Python expertise and produced no output a compliance team could read. Enterprise platforms like Mindgard started at $50,000 per year. There was nothing in the middle.

So we built VULNRA. The same technical depth as the $16,000 engagement. The same compliance output. The same frameworks: EU AI Act, India DPDP, NIST, OWASP, MITRE ATLAS. In 60 seconds. For $49/month.

We built it in India, for founders who look like us — shipping fast, under compliance pressure, without the budget for enterprise vendors. And then we made it work for European DevSecOps teams, US developers, and anyone else who has an AI model in production without knowing if it is safe.

The mission is simple: no developer should ship an unsafe AI model because they couldn't afford to test it.

Principles

What we believe

The opinions that shaped every product decision we've made.

OPEN_BY_DEFAULT
Security tools must be auditable
You cannot trust a security tool you cannot inspect. VULNRA's scan engine, compliance mappings, and probe definitions are MIT licensed and open source. The dashboard is proprietary. The engine never will be.
SELF_SERVE_FIRST
No one should need a sales call to check if their AI is safe
The security industry runs on friction: demos, RFPs, onboarding calls. We built VULNRA to work in 60 seconds with no credit card. The product should sell itself, or it isn't good enough.
PRICE_TRUTH
$16,000 is a market failure, not a pricing strategy
When a seed-stage startup cannot afford to know if their product is safe, that is not a pricing strategy. That is a market failure. We exist to correct it.
INDIA_NATIVE
Built where the next billion users are
India has the largest English-language developer population outside the US, the fastest AI adoption curve, and compliance frameworks that no Western vendor has implemented. We did.
NO_THEATRE
Compliance is only useful if the findings are real
A security report that lists framework citations without actually testing vulnerabilities is legal theatre. Every VULNRA finding is the result of an actual probe executed against your endpoint. No checkboxes.
CONTINUOUS_GUARD
Annual audits are not security
A model that was safe in January can be unsafe in March after a fine-tune. Security is continuous or it is an illusion. Sentinel exists because we believe this deeply.
